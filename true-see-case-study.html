Key Insights

  1. Doctors were more willing to bypass the TRUE-See app and use their smartphones to take & share photos, creating HIPAA risks and extra documentation work.
    (This also brought more clarity to the 59% of users who opened the app & took photos.)
  2. Rapid photo-taking was impossible during high-volume days.

Workflow & Pain Points

Current EHR workflows make photo capture and analysis inefficient.

  1. Sharing photos among doctors & specialists is highly valuable, especially in real-time.
  2. Having to re-take a photo that was misaligned takes up so much time, especially when nurses need to see many patients back-to-back.
  3. Analyzing photos is currently done using the "eyeball test" by doctors.
  4. Viewing two photos side-by-side is not possible with existing EHRs.

Experience Design

The developers and I aligned on Bootstrap very early on in the process.

Why Bootstrap?
  • Both architects were already fluent in Bootstrap (10+ YOE each).
  • Pre-built components like buttons, grids, and navs gave us a solid head start.
  • Bootstrap had a robust UI Kit in Figma to match their implementation 1:1.

Experience Design

I used Cursor to validate and prototype my Bootstrap designs in real code.

  • Catch layout gaps in the designs.
  • Understand real behavior in-browser.
  • Speed up dev handoff by referencing actual Bootstrap class names.

Experience Design

Internal discussions led to further UX updates before conducting our usability tests.

  • Users need to be able to assign Patient AND Wound Type. Can't just be 1 click.
  • We can't technically display wound info here. User has to save session first.
  • Users need the ability to add notes immediately.
  • Sharing might not be possible (or useful) without any data attached to the photo.

Outcomes & Lessons

Our MVP has proven to provide users with a faster, more efficient way to capture, share, and analyze photos.

Key Outcomes & Results
  • Created a HIPAA-compliant photo capture experience that worked independently of EHR restrictions.
  • Reduced the need for reshoots by giving physicians better guidance at the point of capture.
  • Enabled faster collaboration between clinics and specialists by standardizing photo quality and metadata.
What I Learned
  • Healthcare providers need to move very efficiently with the slow & clunky tech they have.
  • Even simple features like photo-taking have deep downstream effects on diagnosis timelines.
  • Strict processes can't slow physicians down, even if that means risking HIPAA to receive faster results.
  • Designing for trust, speed, and context matters just as much as compliance.
